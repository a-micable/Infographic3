Key Impacts
Observable consequences already unfolding globally
🌊
Sea Level Rise
Sea levels have risen 20cm since 1900. At current rates, 1 billion people face coastal flooding by 2100.
🔥
Extreme Heat
Heatwaves are now 5× more likely. 2023 was the hottest year in recorded human history.
🧊
Ice Sheet Loss
Arctic sea ice has declined 13% per decade. Greenland loses 280 billion tonnes of ice annually.
🌿
Biodiversity
1 million species face extinction risk. Coral reefs have declined 50% since the 1950s.
Key Milestones
The climate crisis in time


1958
Keeling Curve Begins
Charles Keeling begins continuous CO₂ measurements at Mauna Loa. CO₂ was 315 ppm. Today it exceeds 420 ppm.
1988
IPCC Founded
The Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change is established to assess scientific evidence on climate change.
1997
Kyoto Protocol
First international treaty binding industrialized nations to reduce greenhouse gas emissions.
2015
Paris Agreement
196 countries agree to limit warming to 1.5°C. Considered the landmark global climate accord.
2023
Hottest Year Ever
2023 shattered all temperature records — every month set a new global heat record for the first time.
